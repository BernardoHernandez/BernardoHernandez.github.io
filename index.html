Current Research

I am currently a Research Associate in Dynamic Modelling and Control for Autonomous Vehicles part of the EU-H2020 Dreams4Cars projects. My research focuses on the estimation of forward models of ground vehicle dynamics for the purpose of forward control design.

Research profile and interest

I hold a PhD from the Automatic Control and Systems Engineering department at the Univesity of Sheffield, UK (2018). My Doctorate Thesis focuses on the design of Model Predictive Control (MPC) based controllers for time-varying, distributed and switching systems.
During 2017 I performed as a Research Assistant at the Rolls-Royce university technology center at the Department of Automatic Control and Systems Engineering at the University of Sheffield.
Prior to my PhD I obtained an MEng in Mechanical Engineering (2013) and a BEng in Aerospace Engineering (2012) from the University of Concepcion, Chile. My masters final project focused on the implementaiton and optimisation of an automatic control platform in a UAV.
My current research interests are in the broad field of model-based control and linear modelling. More particularly in the design of model-based controllers for uncertain and changing systems, where uncertainty may arise from inaccuracies inherent to the modelling process.
